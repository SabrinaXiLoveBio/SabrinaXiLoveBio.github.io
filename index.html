Project-Summary

2019-11-22

The observation of the flagellum structure of the Trypanosoma Brucei under the cryo Electron Tomography

Sabrina Xi

Introduction

Some diseases that caused by the parasites will cause severe harm to human. The most typical example is sleep sickness, a disease caused by T. brucei that threatens millions of people in 36 countries in sub-Saharan Africa. In order to explore the treatment of such diseases, this article explores the flagella of T. brucei as a breakthrough point, using cryo Electron Tomography (cryoET) to analyze and find ways to transform flagella in order to achieve the purpose of treating Africa sleep sickness. This article mainly focuses on the structure of the flagella, the experimental procedure, the application of cryoET and the analysis of the flagella structure that provide the possibility for the treatment of the sleeping sickness.

Materials and Methods

In our study, we used an advanced technique called cryoET to obtain more accurate results. We first placed the test sample on a dedicated electron microscopy grid. The sample needs to be frozen with liquid ethane before it is put into the electron microscope for observation. This allows us to observe samples in different forms. We will use automated plunge freezers to freeze the sample.

When the sample is placed in the observation plate, the grid is tilted one degree each time to get a 3D graph at last. By spreading golden particles randomly onto the sample, we can align the image of tilt series with observing a full picture of the structure of flagellum. The machine allows the sample to be tilted to ±60°, 121 images would be recorded. However, the Electrons can damage the frozen sample, which will affect the tolerance of frozen samples. Therefore, the resolution is about 3-5 nm. When we get the projection images from different angles, we use eTomo and IMOD, tomogram building softwares, to merge the tilt-series of the sample and get a 3D model.

Figure 1A. Above is an axial view of cryo-ET image of T. brucei axoneme. The 9+2 axoneme structure can be seen. Number 1-9 labels all the outer doublet microtubules, and the two circles in the center are central pair apparatus. The outer doublet microtubules are approximately 50nm. On the left side, the PFR structure is also labelled.

Results and discussion

Through the image, we can clearly see the 9+2 axoneme structure of the flagellum. It controls the motility of the T. Brucei. By comparing the image with the model of a flagellum, (Motility and more: the flagellum of Trypanosoma brucei, Gerasimos Langousis1 and Kent L. Hill1,2) we can clearly identify each part of a flagellum.

Figure 1B. On the left hand side is a model of flagellum and PFR structure from the article Motility and more: the flagellum of Trypanosoma brucei, by Gerasimos Langousis1 and Kent L. Hill1,2.

Figure 2. Above is a longitudinal view of the flagellum axoneme of T. Brucei. Part A labelled is a group of three radial spokes of a single axonemal unit. Part B shows the longitudinal perspective of outer doublet microtubule. Part C shows the central pair apparatus. Part D shows the cross section of PFR.

Summary and conclusion

After the observation of the flagellum structure sample through the electron tomography, we found out that the image we took is similar to the model that scientists studied. The 9+2 microtubule structure provides T. Brucei motility to spread disease. The closer observation to the detail of flagellum structure of Trypanosoma Brucei provide us a possibility for the future study about the treatment of Africa sleep sickness by transforming the structure of flagella of T. Brucei. On the other hand, it provides the possibility for further study about the drug targeting to the flagellum on T. Brucei.
